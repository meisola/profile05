Budit

Apr 2020 - Present

Side Project

Overview

Brief: Expense tracking app designed to help people achieve saving goals.

My role: UI, UX, design system, prototype.

Our Problems: My friends and I have tried some app to help us tracking our spending, but some of them are too complex to use with many functions that we don’t need or some are easy to use but can’t fulfil our requirements.

General Problems: Expense tracking would be the first step to manage personal finance. However, people often stop recording their spending after start tracking their expense for a while.

Design opportunity: To design an expense tracking app that provide “just right” functions with simple to use system.

Design solution: Based on the research, we designed a goal function to help people achieve their saving goal.

Process

Research

We first looked into our experience. We all have experience in doing expense tracking. Hence, we noticed some pain points while using expense tracking app and keep recording.

Target Audience


People aged 18 to 28 with simple finance status and want to start understand their expense. Initial target market: Taiwan

Interview


We tried to conduct interview to gather deeper insight about the topic. The interview questions sought to collect information about people’s expense tracking experience, habits, tools and pain points. By designing the questions, I apply the golden circle approach to discover more detail information.

After collected the information, we first do initial coding to reduce and organise with the data for later research. We tried to find out rich information in the conversation by analysing the cleaned data. We found that forgetfulness, laziness and missing receipt happened in most of our interviewees. People sometime did not even know the why they tracking their spending or how to deal with those the report of their finance. Sometimes people may just track their expense because they think it might improve their finance status. I learn that lack of motivation would be central issue that cause people often give up tracking their spending.

Competitive Analysis


We started this process to understand those similar products in Taiwan. I also read some case study form internet to study more information. With those analysis I can have a chance to see what features work well and what are not. Also, I am able to spot the common design patterns that users may be used to. Then, we can figure out something to learn and improve when developing our app.

When studying some money tracking app in Taiwan, we listed some main features. From the analysis, most app provide a main feature is to give users graph to visualise their finance. Some of them provide extremely simple function, only recording expense and show pie chart of spending. Some can connect with bank to syne users’ card, so that they don’t need to input every item they purchase. These both tend to make the recording process as easy as possible. However, only one product tried to help user keep their tracking habit by gamification.

To sum up, many apps try to cut down the time expense tracking cost, so users can input their spending quickly, conveniently and simply. But, few apps focus on helping people to keep the habit and achieve their financial purpose.

Personas

I created personas base on the interview data to help us focus on target users through the whole develop process. In the two personas, they cover our target users setting. One is university student who want to save money for new smartphone. The other is a junior engineer who wants to save some salary every month for future use.

Establish Requirements

We focus on our target users and previous research to develop some key features.

Goal


By setting a goal, people can be motivate to achieve and control their spending. In this case, we will help users to reach their goal by calculate their daily budget.

Project


Create a project can help users better to manage and categorise their expense. Also, users can set a limit expense for the project to help them control their finance on various purpose.

Just Right


We provide no more and no less function, so that users can have enough functions but easy to learn system. Fully functional app may be too complex for first time users or expense-tracking beginner. Hence, we give just enough function with lower threshold.

Information Architecture

After deciding on key features that may help users. I sorted those information form research to structure the product. I built several versions to see which would work more logically. Each category in the picture below would become a tab in the navigation bar.

Low-fidelity Wireframes

Based on the information architecture, I sketched some wireframe to see if the system works. Those wireframes would help guide later stage design and interaction.

Wireframes prototype

I created the prototype with high-fidelity wireframes to test the whole system. With the prototype, we can see if the user flow works smoothly. We discuss every page, so that I can do a fast test with my partner and discover the design may not be useful.

Design System

I created the design system to identify the visual guidelines for this project. Also, this can help our team develop the product easier.

Typography


Our target market is Taiwan, hence, Mandarin and English are both needed for our users.

Colours


Finance may be a serious topic for some people, therefore, we tried to communicate with users gently without giving them intensive experience. I applied various colours saturation to give users different feeling. I used softer colour to present income and expense to decrease stress when see the expense. On the other hand, I used cheering colours on goals and projects, so that it may courage users on achieving their goals.

Icon Set


I created the icon set for users to select when categorising their expense and to let the system more consistent. Not only the default category icons be created, but also several icons made for users to establish custom category.

Current Design

We have completed our UI design and now stepping into evaluation stage.

Goal System


The goal function aims to help users save money by telling them their daily budget. We tell users how much they can spend instead of how much they need to save, so that users only need to stay with in budget. If users are over budget, the budget next day will be cut down, in contrast, if users spend less than the budget, they will get more budget next day.

After users achieve the goal, a congratulations message will sent to users to congrats on their success. The purpose of this is trying to boost users’ sense of achievement and share their joy.

Project System


By creating projects, users can set a limit expense to help them control their spending in different situation. For example, users can create a project for recording their spending when going on a trip, so that recording in project will sperate from other expense. In the project setting, I use the words “limit expense” not “budget” because budget may cause people misunderstand if the budget is related to their income or not. To avoid this confusion, “limit expense” is applied to make users understand the function clearly.

Main Page


Different form most expense tracking app, we do not set the recording page as the main page. To make users first to see the information of their daily budget, I set main page to present this data, also, notification of daily budget will be sent. Moreover, on-going goals and projects will be show in the main page as well. In order to let users record their expense easily and swiftly, a fixed button is provide as a shortcut to record.

Takeaways

Design system and collaborate: At first, we did not thinking about building a visual guideline. However, after few times meeting with another designer, I thought it might be better to have a design system to help us. With the simple system, my partner can apply the guideline on design, so that our design can be more consistent. It can also help us communicate and collaborate.

Breaking down the problems: We were just thinking to make a simple-to-use app at the beginning. After gain deeper insight of problem, we found more details through the research. Then, we can make a better design decisions based on research.

Next Step

We are now carrying out usability test to evaluate our design. At the same time, developer starts coding the structure of app.
